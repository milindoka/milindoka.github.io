The Village of Burambad: Where Land Meets Sea

üåç Geography and Ecosystems

Burambad is situated in the southernmost part of the Konkan coast of Maharashtra, bordered by the lush Western Ghats on one side and the Arabian Sea on the other. This unique position creates three distinct, rich ecosystems:

  • **Coastal/Marine:** Characterized by tidal mudflats, rocky shores, and active fishing grounds, supporting diverse marine life.
  • **Laterite Plateaus:** Elevated areas with classic Konkan laterite soil, perfect for cashew, kokum, and specific endemic flora.
  • **Paddy Fields & Farms:** Valley areas dedicated to rice cultivation and horticulture, forming crucial seasonal wetlands.

ü§ù Community and Traditional Practices

The local community has lived in harmony with the environment for generations, developing profound traditional knowledge. Our PBR captures these practices, which are essential for sustainable living:

Farming

Traditional *Kharif* and *Rabi* cropping methods, focusing on local, hardy rice varieties and rain-fed irrigation.

Medicinal Knowledge

Use of over 50 local plants (like Adulsa and Shisham) by local healers (Vaidyas) for common ailments.
